NS Marks The Spot

Historical maps, live in your pocket.

NS Marks The Spot lets you slide Nova Scotia's Fletcher maps over today's landscape and see old roads, lakes, and landmarks line up under your thumb.

The v1.0 candidate is moving through TestFlight

The public app site presents NS Marks The Spot as a v1.0 candidate moving through TestFlight and App Store readiness work. Treat that as status language from the source site, not a stronger launch claim.

Every slider pull is a little time travel

NS Marks The Spot turns archival Nova Scotia maps into a practical companion for walks, research trips, and weekend detours. Fade between the old survey and the current map to trace where a road bent, where a shoreline shifted, or where a name survived.

Made for maps that do not behave like ordinary screenshots

  • Georeferenced history: Historical raster tiles sit exactly where they belong over the modern map.
  • Instant transparency: A dedicated overlay control makes comparison feel tactile, fast, and readable.
  • POI discoveries: Waterfalls and other points of interest can appear as clean vector layers.
  • Offline-first tiles: Disk and memory caching keep panning smooth when the best places have weak signal.

Open-source cartography

The app is MIT licensed and structured around a map-engine boundary, so the interface can stay steady while the renderer evolves.

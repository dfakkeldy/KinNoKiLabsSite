Services

Repeated operational work, turned into practical software.

The work starts with the real workflow: the quotes, job folders, forms, emails, spreadsheets, supplier notes, and decisions that already keep your business moving.

I build small, reviewable systems around that work — so owners and operators can find what matters, reuse what already exists, and make better decisions with less scrambling.

Workflow Audits

A fixed diagnostic for one operational problem. The output is a clear map of how the work happens today, where time or money leaks out, and what a first useful automation would look like.

The recommendation can be no build. Some workflows need a better checklist or a cleaner source of truth before software is worth the cost.

Single-Workflow Automation Builds

One narrow job at a time: a quote packet, a bid-room checklist, a reporting handoff, a release pipeline — a repeated process with a clear owner.

Not a vague transformation project. A scoped tool that can be reviewed, approved, and improved while the business keeps running.

Reliable Company Knowledge Bases

Your knowledge is already valuable — just scattered across old jobs, spreadsheets, emails, PDFs, and people's heads. I make it findable, reusable, and reviewable before important decisions.

Reliable means source-linked. AI helps with search, extraction, and drafts — but the system shows where information came from, and high-stakes decisions stay with people.

Why it pays

Business memory makes money in practical ways.

Faster quoting — old assumptions, prices, and supplier notes are easier to find for bid/no-bid calls.

Fewer missed requirements — forms, due dates, and compliance docs tied back to real jobs.

Less owner bottleneck — repeated decisions stop living only in one person's memory.

Less rework — the next job starts from reviewed prior knowledge, not a blank spreadsheet.

Better delegation — staff can see the company's preferred way to estimate, document, and close out work.

Better follow-through — schedules, safety paperwork, and closeout requirements stay connected after the win.
